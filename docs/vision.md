# EventLens Vision

This document defines the mission, philosophy, and long-term direction of EventLens.

Tagline:

See the facts. Understand the perspectives. Decide for yourself.

1. Mission
    To build the world's most transparent information platform where users receive facts before narratives, multiple perspectives instead of one, and are empowered to think independently.

2. The Problem
    Modern news platforms optimize for:
    •	Engagement 
    •	Watch time 
    •	Clicks 
    •	Outrage 
    •	Political reinforcement 
    As a result:
    •	Facts are buried under opinions. 
    •	Headlines are written to trigger emotions. 
    •	Algorithms trap users inside ideological bubbles. 
    •	Readers become consumers of narratives instead of investigators of reality. 

3. Our Philosophy
    Truth is complicated.
    Instead of claiming:
    "We know the truth."
    EventLens says:
    "Here's everything currently known."

    Separate observation from interpretation.
    Every event has:
    •	What happened 
    •	What is claimed 
    •	What is verified 
    •	What remains unknown 
    These should never be mixed.

    Users deserve evidence.
    Instead of asking users to trust us,
    we provide:
    •	sources 
    •	timelines 
    •	evidence 
    •	contradictions 
    and let them reach conclusions.

    Transparency beats authority.
    If our AI reaches a conclusion,
    the user should know why.
    Nothing should feel like a black box.

4. Core Goals
    Goal 1
    Present facts before opinions.
    Goal 2
    Separate facts, assumptions, speculation and propaganda.
    Goal 3
    Help users understand events instead of consuming headlines.
    Goal 4
    Reduce algorithmic echo chambers.
    Goal 5
    Reward curiosity instead of outrage.
    Goal 6
    Make learning addictive.

5. Principles
    Facts are sacred.
    Opinions are welcome.
    Confusing the two is not.

    Unknown is acceptable.
    If something isn't known,
    we simply say:
    Unknown.
    Not every story needs an answer today.

    Multiple viewpoints can coexist.
    Two intelligent people may disagree.
    The platform should explain why.

    Sources matter.
    Every important statement should lead back to where it originated.

    Context matters.
    Facts without context often become misinformation.

6. User Experience Goals
    A user should leave EventLens feeling:
    ✅ More informed
    not
    Overwhelmed.

    They should think:
    "I understand this event."
    instead of
    "Everyone is lying."

7. Feed Philosophy
    Not:
    Most Popular
    Not:
    Trending
    Not:
    Most Outrageous
    Instead:
    •	Relevant 
    •	Important 
    •	Diverse 
    •	Personalized 
    •	Educational 

8. Recommendation Philosophy
    Inspired by Spotify.
    Instead of learning:
    User likes Politics.
    Learn:
    •	Reads long investigations 
    •	Prefers international reporting 
    •	Compares multiple viewpoints 
    •	Enjoys scientific articles 
    •	Avoids celebrity gossip 
    •	Reads full articles instead of headlines 
    The algorithm should learn how people learn.

9. Every Event Should Contain
    Summary
    30-second explanation.
    Timeline
    How events unfolded.
    Verified Facts
    Only evidence
    Claims
    Who is saying what.
    Perspectives
    Different interpretations.
    Unknowns
    Questions nobody can answer yet.
    Sources
    Every source used.
    Related Events
    Historical context.

10. AI's Role
    AI should never become:
    "The truth."
    Instead AI becomes:
    •	Research assistant 
    •	Organizer 
    •	Summarizer 
    •	Evidence collector 
    •	Perspective mapper 
    The human always makes the final judgement.

11. Long-Term Vision
    Imagine opening EventLens five years from now.
    Instead of scrolling through hundreds of sensational headlines, you see:
    •	Events that genuinely matter to you. 
    •	Clear timelines instead of fragmented updates. 
    •	Facts separated from interpretation. 
    •	Multiple viewpoints side by side. 
    •	Primary sources one click away. 
    •	A recommendation system that understands your curiosity, not your outrage. 
    The platform doesn't ask you to agree with it. It gives you the tools to understand the world on your own terms.
